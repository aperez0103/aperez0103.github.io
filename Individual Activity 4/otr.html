On The Road: Safety Tips

When riding a motorcycle, safety should always be your top priority. Here are some essential safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Never ride under the influence of alcohol or drugs.
  • Ride Licensed - if riding with a learner's permit, abide by all local restrictions.
  • Always wear a DOT-approved helmet.
  • Wear protective gear, including gloves, jacket, pants, and boots.
  • Check your motorcycle's brakes, lights, and tires before and after each ride.
  • Stay visible to other drivers by wearing bright colors and using reflective gear.
  • Follow all traffic laws and signals.
  • Keep a safe distance from other vehicles on the road.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and anticipate potential hazards.
  • Take a motorcycle safety course to improve your skills.
  • Always ride with both hands on the handlebars and both feet on the footpegs.
  • Use your turn signals to indicate your intentions to other drivers.
  • Be cautious when riding in inclement weather, such as rain or snow.
  • Keep your motorcycle in good working condition by performing regular maintenance.
  • Do not attempt to have a "backpack" (a passenger) without sufficient experience.
  • Practice defensive riding by anticipating the actions of other drivers.
  • Always be prepared for the unexpected, such as potholes or debris on the road.
  • When in doubt, do not be afraid to ask for help.
    • If you suspect an issue with your motorbike, or are confused or unsure about anything regarding your riding, ask an experienced rider. They are the most trustworthy source on the road.

By following these safety tips, you can help ensure a safe and enjoyable riding experience. Remember, safety is not just about wearing gear; it's about being aware of your surroundings and making smart decisions on the road.
